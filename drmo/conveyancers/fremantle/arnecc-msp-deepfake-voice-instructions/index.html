Pre-Settlement Flash Audit for Fremantle Conveyancers: Verify Voice Instructions Against ARNECC Verification of Identity Standards Before Settlement

The phone rings two days before settlement. It’s “the client” — the voice sounds right, the file reference is correct, and they want to confirm a last-minute change to where the trust funds should land. You hang up and something feels off. With consumer-grade voice cloning tools now able to reproduce a recognisable voice from a short recording, conveyancers can no longer treat a familiar voice on a phone call as proof of identity. The Pre-Settlement Flash Audit is a one-shot diagnostic that stress-tests a specific settlement file against the verification obligations Subscribers owe under the ARNECC Model Participation Rules.

Why it matters now

Under the Electronic Conveyancing National Law, Subscribers (including conveyancers acting on PEXA) must comply with the Participation Rules made by the Registrar in each State or Territory. The Australian Registrars’ National Electronic Conveyancing Council (ARNECC) publishes the Model Participation Rules — currently Version 7, published January 2024 — which set out Subscriber obligations around Client Authorisation, Verification of Identity, and the retention of supporting evidence for each electronic conveyancing transaction. Voice-cloning attacks targeting last-minute instruction changes are a recognised threat class: the Australian Cyber Security Centre publishes general guidance on AI-enabled social engineering at https://www.cyber.gov.au/, and ACCC ScamWatch tracks impersonation scams at https://www.scamwatch.gov.au/. Where a Subscriber acts on a deepfake voice instruction without a Verification of Identity step that meets the Rules, the file evidence will not support compliance on subsequent ARNECC compliance examination.

The 5-minute view

  • The ARNECC Model Participation Rules Version 7 (January 2024) is the current published model under the Electronic Conveyancing National Law; each State Registrar (in WA, the Registrar of Titles at Landgate) determines the Participation Rules in force locally
  • Subscribers must verify the identity of their client and retain evidence of that verification for each transaction — a phone call alone, with no documentary support, does not satisfy the Verification of Identity standard
  • Deepfake voice cloning can be produced from short publicly available audio (LinkedIn videos, recorded meetings, voicemail greetings) — voice familiarity is no longer a reliable identity signal
  • The highest-risk window is the final 7 days before settlement, when changes to disbursement instructions are most plausible to settlement staff under time pressure
  • The ACSC recommends out-of-band verification through an independently sourced channel for any instruction change — calling back on a number obtained from the client’s verified identity record, not from the inbound call
  • ARNECC conducts compliance examinations and assessments of Subscribers; insufficient verification evidence on a specific file is a recognised compliance finding
  • A Flash Audit reviews one file’s verification chain against the Rules and identifies the specific gaps before settlement, not after

The Pre-Settlement Flash Audit is a single-transaction diagnostic delivered against one specific settlement file in the Fremantle / Perth metro area.
